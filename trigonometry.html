1. Introduction to Trigonometry

Trigonometry is the study of relationships between angles and sides of triangles. The word comes from "trigon" (triangle) and "metry" (measurement). It has applications in physics, engineering, navigation, and many other fields.

Key Concept: Trigonometry relates angles to ratios of sides in right triangles.

2. Right Triangle Basics

In a right triangle, the three sides are:

  • Hypotenuse: The longest side, opposite the right angle
  • Opposite: The side opposite to the angle of interest
  • Adjacent: The side next to the angle of interest (not the hypotenuse)
Pythagorean Theorem: a¬≤ + b¬≤ = c¬≤
where c is the hypotenuse and a, b are the other sides.
